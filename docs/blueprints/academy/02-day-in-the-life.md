# 02 — Day in the Life: Operational Rhythms

> **Scope:** What a typical day looks like for each role, from opening the
> gate to closing. Not a feature list — a human-centered view of how work
> actually flows.

---

## 1. The Institute Clock

Most coaching institutes operate on a schedule driven by school timings.
The typical window is:

```
06:00  Owner/Manager arrives. Reviews previous day.
07:00  Front desk opens. Counsellors arrive.
07:30  First batch begins (early morning competitive prep)
08:00  Walk-in inquiries start (parents dropping kids on way to work)
10:00  Counsellor follow-up calls begin
12:00  Midday batches. Lunch rotation.
14:00  After-school batches begin (11th/12th, 8th/9th)
16:00  Peak walk-in time (parents leaving work early)
18:00  Evening batches. Fee collection window.
20:00  Last batches end. Metrics generated.
21:00  Owner reviews day. Staff leaves.
```

Weekends (Saturday-Sunday) are peak operation days — more batches, more
walk-ins, more fee collection. Many institutes run 6 days a week.

## 2. Role-by-Role Day

### 2.1 The Owner

**Morning (06:00–08:00)**
- Arrives first. Checks previous day's collection and enrollment numbers.
- Reviews which marketing channels brought yesterday's inquiries. Checks
  cost-per-lead on active campaigns. Asks: "Is the ad spend working?"
- Reviews WhatsApp messages from parents (often personal number).
- Scans staff attendance — is everyone here?
- Quick call with branch manager if multi-branch.

**Mid-morning (08:00–12:00)**
- Meets with counsellors briefly: "How many follow-ups today? Any hot leads?"
- Reviews incoming inquiries from overnight (website forms, missed calls).
- Approves any fee waivers or special discounts.
- Takes a call with a prospective corporate client or school partnership.

**Afternoon (12:00–17:00)**
- Drops into a classroom unannounced — checks if teacher is on time, teaching well.
- Meets a parent who wants to discuss their child's performance.
- Reviews test results from the week. Calls 2-3 parents personally (relationship building).

**Evening (17:00–20:00)**
- Reviews fee collection for the day. Follows up with accountant on pending payments.
- Checks enrollment numbers against monthly target.
- Plans next week's marketing push (new batch starting, special offer).

**End of day (20:00–21:00)**
- Final review: today's numbers, tomorrow's priorities.
- Quick message to staff group: "Good work today. Tomorrow we focus on [X]."

**What the owner worries about:** Cash flow. Enrollment targets. Teacher retention.
Parent complaints. Competition opening nearby.

---

### 2.2 The Branch Manager

**Morning (07:00–09:00)**
- Opens the branch. Checks if all rooms are clean, AC working, boards clean.
- Briefs the team: "Today we have [X] follow-ups, [Y] fee collections pending."
- Checks that all teachers have arrived and batches will start on time.

**Mid-morning (09:00–13:00)**
- Monitors the front desk: are walk-ins being attended to?
- Handles a parent complaint about their child being placed in the wrong batch.
- Coordinates with accountant on pending fee reminders.
- Reviews counsellor performance: who converted, who didn't, why?

**Afternoon (13:00–17:00)**
- Conducts a batch transfer for a student who wants to change timing.
- Reviews test results from the week. Flags 3 students who are slipping.
- Meets with a teacher to discuss curriculum pacing.

**Evening (17:00–20:00)**
- Handles end-of-day fee collection push.
- Checks tomorrow's schedule: any teacher on leave? Any room conflict?
- Updates the owner on today's numbers.

**What the branch manager worries about:** Daily operations running smoothly.
Staff conflicts. Room scheduling. Teacher absenteeism.

---

### 2.3 The Counsellor

**Morning (07:30–10:00)**
- Checks the lead dashboard: 12 new leads from yesterday (website: 5,
  walk-in: 4, referral: 3). Also 8 dormant leads from last month's
  exhibition — 3 showed signs of re-engagement after boards.
- Prioritizes: "These 3 called back yesterday — they're hot. I call them first."
- Makes first follow-up calls. Some parents answer, some don't.
- Sends WhatsApp messages to those who didn't answer: "Hi, following up on
  your enquiry about our JEE program."

**Mid-morning (10:00–13:00)**
- Continues follow-up calls (2nd and 3rd touch for older leads).
- Nurtures a dormant lead: "Mrs. Patel, I remember you were interested in
  NEET last year. We have a new batch starting in April — would you like
  to visit?"
- Conducts a demo class for a parent and student who walked in.
- Handles an objection: "The fees are too high." Proposes installment plan.
- Processes an enrollment: collects documents, generates fee challan.

**Afternoon (13:00–17:00)**
- More follow-up calls. Checks on yesterday's enrollments — did they pay?
- Coordinates with batch manager: "I enrolled 2 students in JEE Morning
  Regular batch. Are there seats?"
- Updates CRM with status of every lead. Marks 2 as "lost" (parent chose
  competitor — records reason). Marks 1 as "enrolled." Marks 3 as
  "dormant — follow up in June."

**Evening (17:00–19:00)**
- Final follow-up calls for the day.
- Prepares tomorrow's call list: who to call, what to say.
- Reviews own performance: 5 calls made, 2 answered, 1 demo, 1 enrollment.

**What the counsellor worries about:** Conversion rate. "Did I follow up fast
enough?" Competition offering lower fees. Parents who ghost after showing interest.

---

### 2.4 The Teacher/Faculty

**Morning (07:00–07:30)**
- Arrives, checks today's teaching schedule.
- Reviews what was taught yesterday — any homework to check?
- Quick chat with branch manager: "Any new students joining my batch today?"

**During class (07:30–14:00, with breaks)**
- Takes attendance (or notes it for someone to mark).
- Teaches. Explains concepts. Solves problems.
- Assigns homework. Mentions upcoming test.

**Afternoon (14:00–17:00)**
- Reviews homework submissions. Marks 15 papers from last week's test.
- Identifies 3 students who are consistently weak — writes a note for the
  branch manager.
- Prepares tomorrow's lesson plan. Creates a practice set.

**Evening (17:00–19:00)**
- Takes an evening batch if scheduled.
- Quick parent interaction: "Ma'am, how is Rahul doing?" — teacher gives
  informal feedback.
- Updates marks in the system (if using software, or writes in a register).

**What the teacher worries about:** Curriculum pace. Student understanding.
Test scores. "Am I teaching well enough?" Administrative work taking
away from teaching time.

---

### 2.5 The Accountant

**Morning (09:00–12:00)**
- Reviews yesterday's collections: 8 payments received, total ₹1,45,000.
- Cross-checks receipts against the register.
- Flags 3 installment payments that were due yesterday — sends reminders.
- Processes a refund request (student leaving mid-course).

**Afternoon (12:00–17:00)**
- Collects fees from walk-in payers. Generates receipts.
- Updates the fee ledger: who has paid, who owes, when is next installment.
- Prepares the bank deposit for cash collected.
- Reviews outstanding payments: ₹8,50,000 across 45 students.

**Evening (17:00–19:00)**
- Generates the day's collection summary for the owner.
- Flags any anomalies: "This student's installment is 15 days late."
- Updates the monthly revenue forecast.

**What the accountant worries about:** Cash matching (does physical cash
match the ledger?). Late payments. Reconciling multiple payment modes.
Generating accurate GST-compliant invoices.

---

### 2.6 The Receptionist / Front Desk

**Morning (07:00–10:00)**
- Opens the front desk. Greets arriving students.
- Answers phone calls: "Hello, [Institute Name], how can I help you?"
- Logs walk-in inquiries in the register or system.
- Directs parents to the counsellor.

**Throughout the day**
- Manages the visitor log.
- Handles basic queries: "What are the batch timings?" "How do I pay fees?"
- Takes messages for staff who are in class.
- Manages the waiting area (water, seating, display boards).
- Distributes study material or notices to students.

**End of day**
- Closes the front desk. Ensures no students are left unattended.
- Hands over any missed-call list to counsellors.

**What the receptionist worries about:** Being the first point of contact.
Making a good impression. Not letting any inquiry slip through.

## 3. Cross-Cutting Rhythms

These are not tied to a single role — they happen across the institute:

### 3.1 The Daily Huddle (5 minutes, morning)
- Owner/Manager + all staff standing in a circle
- "Yesterday: 3 enrollments, ₹1.4L collected. Today: 8 follow-ups pending,
  2 demos scheduled."
- Quick shout-outs or issues. Everyone disperses.

### 3.2 The Weekly Review (30 minutes, Saturday evening)
- Owner + branch manager + counsellor lead + accountant
- Review: enrollments this week vs. target, fee collection vs. target,
  attendance trends, parent complaints, CAC by channel
- Decide: next week's marketing push, any policy changes

### 3.3 The Monthly Board (1st of every month)
- Owner reviews: enrollment trend, revenue trend, retention rate,
  top complaints, staff performance, CAC by channel, renewal readiness
- Decisions: price changes, new batch launches, hiring needs

### 3.4 The Exam Cycle (every 2-4 weeks)
- Test preparation → Test day → Evaluation → Results → Parent communication
- High-stress period for everyone
- Counsellors get more calls ("How did my child do?")

### 3.5 The Admission Cycle (seasonal peaks)
- **April–June:** New academic year. Peak enrollment. All hands on deck.
  This is when the Acquire and Convert capabilities are under maximum load.
- **July–September:** Settling in. Focus on retention and attendance.
- **October–December:** Mid-year assessments. Some dropout risk.
- **January–March:** Board exam prep. Crash courses. High-intensity.
  Renewal conversations for next year begin.

## 4. What the Day Reveals About Software Needs

From observing the day-in-the-life, the software must support:

1. **Speed of follow-up:** Counsellors need lead details instantly —
   on their phone, not at a desk. The 24-hour window matters.
2. **Low friction fee collection:** Accountant needs to generate a receipt
   in 30 seconds, not 5 minutes. UPI QR code > manual bank transfer.
3. **Attendance without ceremony:** Teacher should mark attendance in
   < 30 seconds. Not a 5-minute process.
4. **Parent visibility without effort:** Parents should be able to check
   attendance and results without calling the institute.
5. **Owner dashboard on the phone:** Owner doesn't sit at a desk. They
   need today's numbers on their phone at 6 AM.
6. **Batch flexibility:** Students change batches frequently. The system
   should make this a 2-click operation, not a manual coordination exercise.
7. **Marketing attribution:** Owner needs to know which channels produce
   enrollments, not just leads. CAC per channel, not just total spend.

## 5. Open Questions

1. How do institutes currently handle **teacher leaves**? Is there a
   substitute system, or do classes just get cancelled?
2. What happens when a student **switches courses** mid-way (e.g.,
   JEE → NEET)? How is the fee adjustment handled?
3. Do institutes track **homework completion**, or only test scores?
4. How do teachers currently share **study material** — WhatsApp groups,
   printed handouts, or a platform?
5. Is there a concept of a **trial class** (student attends one class
   before enrolling), and how is that managed?
6. How do **referrals** work — does the referring student/parent get
   a discount, and who tracks that?
